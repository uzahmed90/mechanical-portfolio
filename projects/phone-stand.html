3D Printed Phone Stand

Tools Autodesk Inventor, MakerBot 3D printer

Role Individual project

Project overview

This project is a custom phone stand that I designed and modeled in Autodesk Inventor, then manufactured using the MakerBot 3D printer. The goal was to create a compact stand that supports the phone at a comfortable viewing angle while remaining stable during use.

Objectives

  • Support a smartphone in landscape and portrait orientation.
  • Keep the design simple enough to print without supports on the 3D printer.
  • Provide enough strength and stiffness for everyday use on a desk or nightstand.

Design process

Concept and requirements

I began by listing basic requirements such as minimum opening width for the phone, and target viewing angle. From there I sketched several possible profiles for the stand and selected a design that balanced stability and printability.

CAD modeling in Autodesk Inventor

I created the main geometry as a parametric part in Autodesk Inventor. Key dimensions such as phone thickness, height of the support surface, and base length were defined with parameters so they could be adjusted easily in future iterations.

3D printing considerations

  • Oriented the part so that the layers run in a way that supports bending loads on the stand.
  • Chose layer height, infill percentage, and wall thickness to balance strength and print time.
  • Added small fillets at high stress corners to reduce stress concentration.

Results

The final print met the design goals. The stand holds the phone securely and is comfortable for watching videos or checking notifications. If I rework on the design, I plan to test different angles, add a soft contact surface to protect the phone finish, and make a section for a charging port.
